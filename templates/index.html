What is Alzheimer's Disease?

Alzheimer's Disease (AD) is a neurodegenerative disease and is the most common cause of dementia. It is an incredibly costly disease, and over 30 million people are afflicted with AD. Life expectancy from time of diagnosis is 3-10 years. 1-in-10 people over the age of 65 have AD, and only 1-in-4 people with AD have actually been diagnosed. Of the 10 leading causes of death in the United States, AD is the only one that cannot be cured or slowed.

Project Goals

  • Predict the diagnosis of each patient
  • Predict patients that are at risk
